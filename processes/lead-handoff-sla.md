# Marketing to Sales Lead Handoff SLA

A Service Level Agreement (SLA) defining how and when Marketing hands off
qualified leads to Sales, and how Sales commits to following up.

---

## 🎯 Purpose

A Lead Handoff SLA exists to:
- Eliminate leads falling through the cracks
- Set clear expectations between Marketing and Sales
- Improve speed-to-contact and conversion rates
- Create accountability on both sides of the funnel

---

## 🔁 The Handoff Process

### Step 1 — Lead Qualifies as MQL
Marketing qualifies a lead as a **Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)** when:

- [ ] Lead score reaches threshold (e.g. 50+ points)
- [ ] Lead has engaged with 2+ marketing touchpoints
- [ ] Lead fits Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) criteria
- [ ] Lead is not an existing customer or competitor

---

### Step 2 — Marketing Notifies Sales
Once MQL criteria are met:

- CRM automatically assigns lead to the correct Sales rep
- Sales rep receives automated notification via CRM
- Lead record is populated with:
  - Full contact details
  - Company information
  - Engagement history (emails opened, pages visited, forms filled)
  - Lead score breakdown
  - Original lead source (UTM data)

---

### Step 3 — Sales Accepts or Rejects the Lead

| Action | Timeframe | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sales reviews lead | Within 4 business hours | Check CRM record |
| Sales accepts lead | Within 8 business hours | Move to SQL stage |
| Sales rejects lead | Within 8 business hours | Must provide rejection reason |

**Valid rejection reasons:**
- Bad contact information
- Existing customer
- Outside target market
- Duplicate record

---

### Step 4 — Sales Follow-Up Commitment

Once a lead is accepted as an **SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)**:

| Touchpoint | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| First outreach attempt | Within 1 business day |
| Second attempt (if no response) | Within 3 business days |
| Third attempt (if no response) | Within 5 business days |
| Mark as nurture or disqualify | After 3 failed attempts |

---

## 📊 SLA Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| MQL to SQL conversion rate | > 30% |
| Lead acceptance rate | > 80% |
| Speed to first contact | < 24 hours |
| Lead rejection rate | < 20% |
| Leads recycled back to nurture | Tracked monthly |

---

## 🔄 Recycled & Rejected Leads

Leads rejected by Sales are **not discarded**. They are:
- Moved back into a nurture workflow
- Re-evaluated after 30/60/90 days
- Reviewed in monthly Marketing & Sales sync

---

## 📅 Review Cadence

This SLA should be reviewed:
- **Monthly** — Marketing & Sales sync meeting
- **Quarterly** — Full SLA review and threshold adjustment
- **Annually** — Full process audit
